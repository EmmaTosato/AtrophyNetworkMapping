# Brain Journal Style Guide üß†

This document contains the *strict* style rules for the journal *Brain*. **All output text for the paper must rigorously adhere to these guidelines.**

## 1. üÜé Abbreviations
*   **General Rule**: Avoid abbreviations unless very well known (e.g., DNA) or for gene/protein symbols.
    *   Limit to as few as possible, only for repeatedly used words.
    *   **Definition**: Spell out at first mention + (Abbrev). Use abbreviation thereafter.
    *   **Legends**: Define **ALL** abbreviations in every Figure/Table legend, even if defined in text.
*   **Allowed in Title/Abstract** (Commonly Accepted):
    *   AIDS, DNA, mRNA, cDNA, RNA, tRNA
    *   ANOVA
    *   ATP
    *   CNS, CSF
    *   CT, MRI, PET
    *   ECG, EEG, EMG
    *   ELISA, PCR
    *   GABA
    *   GP (doctor)
    *   HIV
    *   PBS
    *   REM

## 2. ‚úçÔ∏è Spelling & Grammar
*   **English Variant**: **Oxford English Spelling**.
    *   Use **-ize** suffixes (e.g., *organize*, *summarize*, *characterize*).
    *   **Exception**: *analyse*, *paralyse*.
    *   Use *behaviour*, *colour*, *signalling*.
*   **Clauses**:
    *   **Restrictive**: Use **'that'** (essential info, no comma). *The book that has a red cover is mine.*
    *   **Non-restrictive**: Use **'which'** (extra info, with comma). *The book, which has a red cover, is mine.*
*   **Capitalization**:
    *   Capitalize after a colon.
    *   Capitalize classifications + number: *Experiment 1*, *Patient 5*, *Day 2*, *Case 3*.
*   **Terminology**:
    *   Use **Male/Female** (not Men/Women).
    *   Use **"Most"** (avoid *"the vast majority"*).
    *   Reserve **"Parameters"** only for scientific measurements.
    *   Avoid **"and/or"**. Use 'and', 'or', or 'either or both'.
    *   **Drugs**: Use **generic names**. If proprietary brand used, include brand in parentheses once in Methods. No supplier location needed.

## 3. üî¢ Numbers & Units
*   **Numbers < 10**: **Spell out** (one, two, ... nine). *Includes ordinals (first, ninth).*
*   **Numbers ‚â• 10**: Use **Arabic numerals** (10, 11, ...).
*   **Exceptions (Always Arabic)**:
    *   Units of measure (e.g., *5 mg*, *3 years*).
    *   Designators (*Case 5*, *Day 2*).
    *   Scores, Confidence Intervals.
    *   Fractions < 1 (spell out? Context implies spell out if general, but usually decimals preferred in science). *Guide says "fractions less than one" spelled out.*
*   **Sentence Start**: **Always spell out** numbers at the start of a sentence.
*   **Dates**: Day Month Year (e.g., *1 January 2006*).
*   **Ranges**: Use **en-dash (‚Äì)** closed up (no spaces).
    *   *pp. 23‚Äì36*, *1939‚Äì45*.
    *   **Repeat symbols** if closed up: *4%‚Äì10%* (not 4‚Äì10%), otherwise *2‚Äì3 mg*.
*   **Confidence Limits (95%)**: Use comma to separate values: *(15, 28)*, **not** *(15-28)*.

## 4. üõë Punctuation
*   **Oxford Comma**: **DO NOT USE** (e.g., write "red, white and blue", NOT "red, white, and blue").
*   **En-dash (‚Äì)** (longer than hyphen `-`):
    *   **Ranges**: *4%‚Äì10%*.
    *   **Connections/Relations**: *Dover‚ÄìCalais crossing*, *AD‚ÄìPSP comparison*, *Mann‚ÄìWhitney U-test*, *editor‚Äìauthor relationship*.
    *   **Parentheticals**: *he was‚Äîso he said‚Äîa businessman* (closed up).
*   **Em-dash (‚Äî)**: Use to introduce a phrase or replace a colon (less formal).
*   **Abbreviations**:
    *   **e.g.** and **i.e.** : **NO comma** after them.
*   **Respectively**: Preceded by a comma (*...values were X and Y, respectively*).
*   **Parentheses**: Order is `{[()]}`. e.g. *{[(...)]}*.

## 5. üìä Statistics & Significance
*   **"Significant"**:
    *   **NEVER** write "statistically significant".
    *   **NEVER** use both a P-value and "significant" for the same point.
    *   Reserve "significant" **only** for statistical testing.
*   **P-values**:
    *   Format: **Upper case P**, **Italics**, **Romand operators**.
    *   Example: ***P* < 0.001**, ***P* = 0.04**.
    *   Asterisks in tables: \* *P* ‚â§ 0.05, \*\* *P* ‚â§ 0.01, \*\*\* *P* ‚â§ 0.001.
*   **Correlations**: Do not say "positive/negative" or "inverse". Describe the observation (e.g., *"an increase in X was associated with..."*).
*   **Equations**:
    *   Simple equations in word format. Complex in MathType.
    *   **Multiplication**: Use `√ó` (not *). e.g. *6 √ó 10^5* (not 6E5).
    *   **F-values**: *F*(2,35) = ...
    *   **t-test**: *t* (lower case, italic).
    *   **U-test**: *U* (Roman U, e.g. *Mann‚ÄìWhitney U-test*).

## 6. üìù Formatting & Citations
*   **Italics**:
    *   **NO** italics for emphasis (use single quotes `‚Äò...‚Äô`).
    *   **Use for**:
        *   Book/Periodical titles.
        *   Latin phrases (*in situ*, *et al.*, *per se*).
        *   Species names (*E. coli*).
        *   Variables (*P*, *t*, *n*).
    *   **Do NOT use for**: Software names, Restriction enzymes.
*   **Quotes**:
    *   Use **single quotes** (`‚Äòtext‚Äô`). Double quotes only for quotes within quotes.
    *   $> 5$ lines: Separate paragraph, indented, smaller font.
*   **Lists**:
    *   Run-in format: *(i) item; (ii) item; and (iii) item*.
*   **Citations (Figures)**:
    *   **Format**: *Fig. 1*, *Figs 3‚Äì6*, *Fig. 2A and B*, *Fig. 3A‚ÄìC*, *Fig. 4A(i and ii) and B*.
    *   **Supplementary**: Cite *after* main text figures.
        *   *Fig. 2A and B and Supplementary material*
        *   *Figs 5 and 6, Supplementary Fig. 3 and Supplementary Tables 1‚Äì7*
    *   **Avoid** repetition in the same paragraph.
*   **Author Names in Text**:
    *   **Avoid** unless historical (Darwin, Newton).
    *   **Incorrect**: *Smith et al. reported...*
    *   **Correct**: *Similar findings have been reported [citation #]*
    *   **Reviews**: *For reviews, see Al-Chalabi et al.^4* (generalized statement allowed for reviews).

## 7. üìÑ Manuscript Preparation

### Article Types & Limits

| Type | Word Limit* | Abstract | References | Display Items |
|------|-------------|----------|------------|---------------|
| Original Article | 6000 | 400 words | No limit | 8 |
| Report | 2500 | 200 words | 30 | 4 |
| Review | 9000 | 400 words | No limit | 8 |
| Update | 3000 | 200 words | No limit | 4 |
| Essay | 2000 | None | 10 | 2 |
| Letter to Editor | 1500 | None | 10 | 2 |
| Opinion | 2000 | 1 sentence strap line | 10 | 2 |

*\* Excluding abstract, title page, tables, figure legends and references.*

### Formatting Requirements

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Font | 12-point, Times New Roman, justified |
| Line spacing | 1.5 or double-spaced |
| Margins | 2.5 cm (Normal) |
| Page size | A4 (21 √ó 29.7 cm) |
| Title (full) | Max 100 characters (including spaces) |
| Title (running) | Max 40 characters (including spaces) |
| Headings | Max 100 characters |

*   **No abbreviations in titles** (except accepted gene symbols).
*   **No page or line numbers**.

### Required Sections (in order)

1.  Title page
2.  Abstract
3.  Introduction
4.  Materials and methods
5.  Results
6.  Discussion
7.  Data availability
8.  Acknowledgements
9.  Funding
10. Competing interests
11. Supplementary material
12. References

### Abstract

*   **Structure**: Background, Methods, Results, Conclusion (each on new line, **no subtitles**).
*   Include: number of subjects, controls, age range, gender, statistical evidence.
*   **No abbreviations** except accepted gene symbols and the allowed list (see Section 1).
*   **Keywords**: Up to 6 (not words already in title/abstract).

### Authors & Affiliations

*   Format: **First name, Middle initial, Last name** (e.g. John E. Smith).
*   Include ORCID iD if registered.
*   One corresponding author (max two after acceptance).
*   **AI/LLM use**: Must be documented in Methods/Acknowledgements. AI cannot be credited as author.

### References (AMA Style, 11th Edition)

*   **In-text**: Superscript numbers after punctuation (period, comma, quotation mark, parenthesis). Inside semicolons/colons.
*   No space before superscript.
*   Sequential numbering; same source keeps same number.
*   Multiple citations: commas for separate, hyphens for sequential (e.g. `1,3,6-9`).
*   Author in text: `Smith1`, `Smith and Watson2`, `Smith et al.3`

**Reference List Format**:
*   Max 6 authors. If >6: first 3 + *et al.*
*   Journal: `Author(s). Title. *Journal*. Year;Vol:Pages.`
*   Book: `Author(s), eds. *Title*. Publisher; Year.`
*   Chapter: `Author. Chapter title. In: Editor, ed. *Book*. Publisher; Year:Pages.`

### Tables

*   **Format**: Editable Word file (no images/PDFs).
*   **Font**: **Gill Sans MT**, **8 pt**.
*   **Headings**: Bold, short. Super-headings centered.
*   **Spacing**: Single spacing, cell height 0.45 cm (0.18").
*   **Margins**:
    *   *Portrait*: Top/Bottom 1.8 cm, Left/Right 3.1 cm.
    *   *Landscape*: Top/Bottom 3.1 cm, Left/Right 1.8 cm.
*   **Prohibitions**: No colour/shaded cells. No tab-separated tables. No vertical lines usually (implied by example).
*   **Footnotes**: Superscript letters ($^a$, $^b$) listed alphabetically (left-to-right, top-to-bottom).
*   **Statistics**: Symbols for *P*-values (e.g. $*P < 0.05$). Bold for significant values.
*   **References in Table**: AMA style numbers ($^{23}$). No separate ref list.

### Figures

*   **Legends**: At end of main text.
*   **Panel Labels**: **Bold** in legend (**A**, **B**).
*   **Resolution**: 300 dpi (colour/halftone), 600 dpi (line art).
*   **Requirements**:
    *   **Magnification bar** mandatory for micrographs.
    *   Axes must not extend beyond data range.
    *   Leading zeros for decimals (0.1, not .1).
    *   Symbols/lines minimal size: 5 pt / 0.5 pt.
    *   Distinguishable symbols/lines.
    *   Colour-blind friendly.

### Manuscript General Formatting

*   **Font**: **Times New Roman**, **12 pt**, Use **Justified** alignment.
*   **Spelling**: Oxford English Dictionary (British English, e.g. *analyse*).
*   **Title**: No paragraph breaks.
*   **Abstract**: Max 400 words. Background/Methods/Results/Conclusion on new lines but **NO subtitles**. No abbreviations (except gene/protein).
*   **Keywords**: Max 6, separated by semi-colon.
*   **Abbreviations**: Define at first use. None in Abstract.
*   **Citations**: AMA 11th. Superscript numbers *inside* colons/semicolons (`abc;$^{12}`). *After* commas/periods.

### Punctuation & Symbols

*   **Hyphen (-)**: Compound words, prefixes (follow *Concise Oxford Dictionary*).
    *   Examples: *multi-scale*, *non-restrictive*.
*   **En-dash (‚Äì)**:
    *   **Ranges** (closed up, no spaces): *pp. 23‚Äì36*, *1939‚Äì45*, *4%‚Äì10%*.
    *   **Connections/Relations**: *Blood‚ÄìBrain barrier*, *Mann‚ÄìWhitney U-test*, *Dover‚ÄìCalais*.
    *   **Parenthetical phrases**: Closed up (e.g. *he was‚Äîso he said‚Äîa businessman*).
*   **Em-dash (‚Äî)**: Use sparingly for afterthoughts or replacing colons.
*   **Relative Clauses**:
    *   **That**: Restrictive clauses (essential info). *The book that has a red cover is mine.*
    *   **Which**: Non-restrictive clauses (extra info, usually after comma). *The book, which has a red cover, is mine.*

### Data & Code Availability

*   **Mandatory Statement**: Must include hyperlinks to public data (or explanation).
*   **Code**: If applicable, provide availability (e.g. GitHub link).

### Supplementary Material

*   Max **10 files**, max **15 MB each**.
*   Combine into single PDF if possible.
*   Images: max 640 √ó 480 pixels.
*   Video: .mp3 (audio), .mpg/.mp4 (video).
*   **Not edited by journal** ‚Äì ensure clarity and no tracked changes.

### Statistical Analysis Section

Required content:
*   Statistical test applied (e.g. two-sample *t*-test, ANCOVA).
*   Test statistic (*t*, *U*, *F*, *r*).
*   Degrees of freedom.
*   **Exact *P*-value**.
*   One- or two-tailed indication.
*   Alpha-level (e.g. *P* < 0.05).
*   **95% confidence intervals encouraged**.
*   Blinding and randomization details (or justification if not performed).
*   A priori sample size calculation (power, alpha, effect size, source of SD).

### Patient Consent

*   Record consent per Declaration of Helsinki.
*   State ethical committee approval.
*   Obtain consent for identifiable photographs/details.

### Gene Nomenclature

*   Use HGNC-approved symbols for human genes.
*   **Gene symbols**: Italicized (*IGF1*).
*   **Protein symbols**: Not italicized (IGF1).
*   Gene names written in full: Not italicized (*insulin-like growth factor 1*).
